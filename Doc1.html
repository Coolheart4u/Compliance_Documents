Research Summary: The Costs of Circumventing Tariffs

Authors: Jaya Wen, Ebehi Iyoha, Edmund Malesky, and Sung-Ju Wu

Published: February 13, 2025

Source: Harvard Business Review

Key Insights

  • Chinese exporters used Vietnam to reroute goods and bypass U.S. tariffs during the 2018 trade war.
  • Researchers analyzed trade at country, province, and firm levels for accuracy.
  • Province-level data provides the most realistic estimate of rerouting activity.
  • Majority of rerouting was done by Chinese-owned firms in Vietnam.
  • Rerouted exports reached up to $6.3B (province-level), showing significant circumvention.

Policy & Business Recommendations

  • Broad, country-based tariffs are ineffective and lead to increased consumer costs.
  • Targeted, firm-level sanctions are more precise and efficient.
  • U.S. companies must scrutinize foreign suppliers to avoid customs risks.
  • More detailed data collection is needed to distinguish between real production and rerouting.
