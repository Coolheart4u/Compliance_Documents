Reevaluation of High-Risk Individuals and Entities

Objective of the Reevaluation

The process of reevaluating individuals or entities identified as high risk is critical to maintaining the integrity and security of financial institutions. This includes individuals who held prominent positions as Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and those residing in countries listed on the FATF grey list. Reevaluation ensures ongoing compliance with international standards and effective risk management.

Key Information to Collect

The following details should be gathered to properly assess the risk associated with an individual or entity:

  • Personal and Employment History: Determine the current and past roles of the individual, especially in government, state-owned enterprises, or military positions.
  • Source of Wealth: Understanding the origin of an individual’s wealth helps assess any potential illicit financial flows.
  • Geopolitical Risk Assessment: Review the risk associated with the individual’s country of residence, particularly if it is listed on the FATF grey list.
  • Ongoing Political Exposure: For individuals who no longer hold a prominent PEP position, assess whether they maintain influential relationships or access to public office.
  • Family and Close Associates: Evaluate the political and financial profiles of close family members and associates who may pose indirect risks.

Example of Key Information to Ask

Example 1: “Can you provide an overview of your political and professional history, including any past roles in government or high-level public office?”

Example 2: “How did you accumulate your wealth, and can you provide supporting documentation or evidence of legitimate sources?”

Example 3: “Are there any ongoing business interests or relationships with individuals in the current government or public sectors?”

Example 4: “Can you clarify your relationship with individuals who hold or have held prominent political or business positions?”

Steps to Conduct a Thorough Risk Evaluation

When reevaluating high-risk individuals and entities, ensure the following steps are followed:

  • Step 1: Gather and verify comprehensive background information on the individual or entity.
  • Step 2: Conduct an in-depth review of their financial activities, including transactions, investments, and partnerships.
  • Step 3: Cross-reference with local and international sanctions lists, PEP databases, and FATF grey list information.
  • Step 4: Analyze the geographical risk factors of their country of residence or operations.
  • Step 5: Assess ongoing business relationships that may represent elevated risks due to the individual’s political ties or status.
  • Step 6: Regularly update the risk profile based on new intelligence, changes in political landscapes, or shifts in financial activity.

Documentation and Reporting

Ensure all findings from the reevaluation process are documented thoroughly. This includes:

  • Detailed Reports summarizing the evaluation process and conclusions.
  • Supporting Documentation such as financial statements, transaction histories, and evidence of wealth origins.
  • Ongoing Monitoring Plans to reassess risk profiles periodically or in response to major events.
