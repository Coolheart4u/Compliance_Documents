Compliance Issues in Financial Statements: Resources & Examples

1. Regulatory & Standards-Based Resources

U.S. GAAP & SEC Filings

  • SEC’s EDGAR Database - Access 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K filings, auditor notes, and restatements.
  • FASB Accounting Standards Codification - Authoritative source for U.S. GAAP.

IFRS Standards (Europe & Global)

  • IFRS Foundation - Full access to international accounting standards and examples.
  • ESMA - Interactive Single Rulebook - EU guidance on IFRS application.

2. Practical Guides with Real Examples

  • Book: Financial Shenanigans by Howard Schilit - Real-world earnings manipulation cases.
  • Manual: PwC's Manual of Accounting (IFRS or GAAP)
  • SEC Comment Letters: Found via SEC Search - Issues like improper revenue recognition.

Example: A company using bill-and-hold arrangements without satisfying IFRS 15's control transfer requirements could face compliance issues.

3. Red Flag Checklists & Tools

  • AICPA Audit Guides - Include industry-specific compliance checklists.
  • Calcbench - Offers anomaly detection in SEC filings.
  • XBRL Analytics - Use XBRL tags to detect irregularities.

Example: XBRL analysis revealing off-balance sheet leases not discussed in MD&A.

5. Is Using 3 years old Financials for Investment Evaluation OK?

Generally not OK unless you're doing historical analysis. For investment, use latest data (2023 or TTM).

  • Markets changed due to inflation, interest rates, and geopolitical events.
  • Post-COVID structural shifts in operations and debt levels.
  • Use supplementary data: earnings calls, press releases, analyst updates.

Tip: Get recent EU financials via:

  • CONSOB (Italy) – Financial reports of Italian listed companies
  • Bundesanzeiger (Germany) – Official German company filings
  • UK Companies House – Free access to UK filings
